## Overview
The game spawns atoms of 4 elements (hydrogen, carbon, nitrogen, and oxygen) drifting across the screen. Using drag-and-drop, these can be placed in the Lab, a draggable subwindow for doing chemistry. There, they are bonded with each other to score points. Points can also be spent to undo bonds between atoms. Bonus points are awarded for completing molecules. The game is over when the time is up- score and corresponding medal awarded is determined from the point total on game over.

### Possible extra mechanics to add with spare time:
- **Bounty** of random molecules will be assigned. Create and submit the assigned molecules to earn lots of points.
- **Chemist Mode** (hard mode): Molecules and radicals will spawn instead of bare atoms, requiring you to spend points to undo their bonds.
  - For increased difficulty and realism: follow Crown Jewels’s scoring system, where bonus points from molecule completion are separate and cannot be used to undo bonds.
- **Lab upgrades**: Initial Lab space is very limited, spend points to expand the Lab.

## Expected Outcome
An interactive chemistry game improving upon Crown Joule’s premise: demonstrating that forming bonds releases energy (points), and breaking bonds uses energy. It should have UI/UX designed to onboard chemistry newbies with little tutorial needed, while providing efficiency and convenience for experienced players. It should also feature additional mechanics like the drifting atoms and the time limit to further gamify it.
